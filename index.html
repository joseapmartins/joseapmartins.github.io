About

I am José A. Martins, a polymer physicist. I’m 64 and I like simple, careful work. I’ve published fewer papers than many colleagues because I’ve spent a long time wrestling with a few hard problems — melt memory, what an entanglement really is, and how local order governs kinetics.

If you prefer substance over volume, we’ll get along.

Research

What is an entanglement?

Intramolecular definition from aligned (ACS) and random (RCS) sequences; links to plateau modulus and reptation.

Melt memory & crystallization

Kinetics under quiescent and flow; temperature calibration and true sample temperature.

Kuhn segments & dynamics

Conformational substructures, stretched relaxation, anomalous diffusion.

Instrumentation

Shear DTA; rheometer & DSC calibration; measuring what we think we measure.
